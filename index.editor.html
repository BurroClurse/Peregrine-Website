What sets it apart

and tells you why you missed.

Drift diagnosis tracks each laser pulse from start to finish and shows how your grip or trigger press moved the muzzle at the instant the shot broke. Peregrine reads the movement, names the likely fault — flinch, trigger jerk, heeling, the wrong fingers — and gives you the correction to try next.

  • Every clock position maps to a known cause and fix
  • Ex: 6 o'clock anticipates recoil; 12 o'clock lifts the sight early
  • A live prompt appears when a pattern shows in 3 of your last 5 shots

How it works

Three steps to begin.

  1. 01

    Print a target

    Pick a target and print it. The corner markers tell Peregrine its size and how far away it sits.

  2. 02

    Set up & choose a drill

    Prop your phone, frame the target, and pick a drill or free-shoot. Set a par time if you want the pressure.

  3. 03

    Dry-fire & review

    Run the session. Peregrine scores each hit and times it, then saves the whole session to your history.

What you need

The kit for a session.

Most of it you already own. Confirm your firearm is unloaded and keep live ammunition out of the room.

  • Firearm

    Required

    The handgun you train with. Follow the maker's dry-fire guidance, and skip rimfire unless it's rated for dry fire. Semiautos are easiest — the slide resets the trigger.

  • Laser training cartridge

    Required

    A red laser cartridge in your caliber. It sits in the chamber and flashes when the trigger breaks — that flash is the hit Peregrine scores.

  • Printer

    Required

    Any home printer, standard letter paper. Keep the QR code and corner markers sharp and flat so the app can register the target.

  • Tripod or phone stand

    Highly recommended

    Holds your phone steady near target height, so framing stays put for the whole session.

  • TRT dry-fire insert

    Nice to have

    A magazine insert that resets most semiauto triggers, so you can run strings without racking the slide. 3D-print files are included in the app.
