What sets it apart

It tells you why you missed.

Drift diagnosis tracks each laser pulse from start to finish and shows how your grip or trigger press moved the muzzle at the instant the shot broke. Peregrine reads the movement, names the likely fault — flinch, trigger jerk, heeling, the wrong fingers — and gives you the correction to try next.

  • Every clock position maps to a known cause and fix
  • Ex: 6 o'clock anticipates recoil; 12 o'clock lifts the sight early
  • A live prompt appears when a pattern shows in 3 of your last 5 shots

Other apps tell you just when and where. Peregrine tells you why.

What you need

The kit for a session.

Most of it you already own. Confirm your firearm is unloaded and keep live ammunition out of the room.

  • Firearm

    Required

    The handgun you train with, paired with a matching laser cartridge. Confirm it's unloaded, keep live ammunition out of the room, and follow the maker's dry-fire guidance. Don't dry fire rimfire guns unless the maker says it's safe. Semiautos are easiest — the slide resets the trigger; revolvers may need the cylinder rotated back to the cartridge between shots.

  • Laser training cartridge

    Required

    A red laser cartridge in the correct caliber. It sits in the chamber and flashes on the target when the striker or hammer drops — Peregrine scores that flash as the hit.

  • Printer

    Required

    Print targets from the Targets tab on standard letter paper. Keep the QR code and black corner markers sharp, flat, and uncovered so the app can register the target.

  • Tripod or phone stand

    Highly recommended

    A stable stand that holds the camera near target height. An adjustable tripod makes framing easier and keeps the phone from moving during a session.

  • TRT dry-fire insert

    Nice to have

    A magazine insert that lets many semiauto triggers reset between presses, so you can run strings without racking the slide every time. 3D-printable files are included in the app.
